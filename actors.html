Sean Connery

Sir Thomas Sean Connery who was born on the 25th August 1930 is a retired Scottish actor and producer. He was the first actor to play James bond in the film franchise in 1962, going on to star in seven more between 1962 and 1983. Connery's acting career also includes such films as Marnie, The Name of the Rose, The League of Extraordinary Gentlemen, Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade, The Hunt for Red October, Finding Forrester, Highlander, Murder on the Orient Express, Dragonheart, and The Rock.



David Niven

In 1965 Niven was signed by producer Charles Feldman to play James Bond in Casino Royale, a film not made by Eon Productions. Niven was 56 when he played Bond[54] and his characterisation was that of an elderly man who had won the Victoria Cross at the Siege of Mafeking.



George Lazenby

George Robert Lazenby was born September 5th 1939 in Goulburn, New South Wales, Australia, to Sheila Joan (Bodel) and George Edward Lazenby. After serving in the Australian army George moved to London in 1964 then in 1968, he was cast as "James Bond". Until then his only previous acting experience was in commercials, and his only film appearance was a small role in a 1965 Italian-made Bond spoof. Lazenby won the role based on: a screen-test fight scene, the strength of his interviews, fight skills and audition footage.



Roger Moore

Roger George Moore was born on October 14 1927 in Stockwell, London, England. The son of Lillian (Pope) and George Alfred Moore who was a policeman. At first Moore wanted to be an artist, however got into films full time after becoming an extra in the late 1940s. Moore also served in the British military during the Second World War. Live and Let Die (1973) was his first Bond movie which earned more outside of America than Diamonds Are Forever (1971); Connery's last outing as James Bond. He went on to star in another six Bond films between 1973 and 1985.



Timothy Dalton

After leaving Herbert Strutt Grammar School at age 16, Timothy toured as a leading member of Michael Croft's National Youth Theater. Between 1964 and 1966 he studied at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art (RADA) and just before completing his two years, he quit and joined the Birmingham Repertory Theatre. Here, he played the lead in many productions under the direction of Peter Dews while at the same time playing the part of James Bond in The Living Daylights (1987) and Licence to Kill (1989), and even more brutally, recently, as Rhett Butler turning professional.



Pierce Brosnan

His most popular role is that of British secret agent James Bond. He was the fifth actor to portray Bond and starred in 4 films between 1995 and 2002. The death of Cassandra Harris, his wife of eleven years in 1991 left him with three children Christopher and Charlotte (who died in 2013) from Cassandra's first marriage and Sean from their marriage. Since her death, he has had two children with his second wife - Keely Shaye Brosnan.



Daniel Craig

One of the British theatre's most famous actors; Daniel Craig, who waited tables as a struggling teenage actor with the National Youth Theatre went on to star as James Bond in Casino Royale (2006) made by Eon Productions. He then starred in 3 other Bond films: Quantum of Solace (2008), Skyfall (2012), Spectre (2015). Craig will also play James Bond in the upcoming 'Bond 25' in 2020.
